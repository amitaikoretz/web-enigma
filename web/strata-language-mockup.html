New language proposal

Strata

Strata is a UI language built around layered planes, directional cuts, and banded information. It avoids the familiar “card + rounded button” vocabulary and instead treats interface as a composed surface: framed fields, editorial strips, and asymmetric modules that feel printed, anchored, and tactile.

Ambient control

Secondary surfaces are softer and cooler, but still clipped and structured. The result feels less like a product dashboard and more like an instrument panel made for quiet environments.

Stack

Events are bands, not cards Recent activity is rendered as a stacked ribbon system. Each band has its own thickness, weight, and left edge cut, making importance visible from shape alone.
Controls are instruments Buttons are treated as calibrated tools, not rounded tokens. Primary actions span wider, secondary actions stay narrow and structural.
Whitespace is a structural material Empty space is distributed like load-bearing material, separating zones by pressure rather than just margin.

Behaviors

Active context snaps to the margin The current area is marked at the edge of the screen and inside the current field, so orientation is always visible without relying on tabs.
Information prefers asymmetry Every surface is offset slightly. The composition feels hand-arranged but still systematic.
Accent is rare and functional Color is not the primary organizing tool. It appears only when something needs urgency, priority, or emphasis.

Phase 01

Establish the spine. Navigation and global state live along the edge, with a hard line and stable rhythm.

Phase 02

Stack the field. Related content forms banded layers, with each band varying in thickness and opacity.

Phase 03

Reveal the action. Primary tasks emerge as a strong plate while tertiary tasks collapse into narrow instruments.

Strata deliberately avoids the familiar Apple-HIG or Material vocabulary of rounded cards, floating surfaces, and bubbly controls. It is built as a layered board system: clipped panels, edge-led navigation, and editorial bands.
